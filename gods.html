Gods have played a huge role in the Norse Mythology. It's impossible to tell how many of them there were. Some sources will go with number 9, in some texts we can see at least 20 mentioned, and the further we dig, the higher amount we get. In total there might be even hundreds of them but, on the other hand, many of Gods were assigned to multiple names. That is because all the myths were passed down orally.

About Gods

Gods in the Norse Mythology were spiritual beings with human-like bodies and personalities but with unordinary powers. Many of them travelled throughout the world, and therefore could be seen by ordinary people, even when they didn't recognize them. For example, Odin (the Wanderer) had tendency to travel through the land undercover and put people to the test. Sometimes he could be a rich merchant, other times poor rogue who needed help.

From what we know, there were two groups of Gods - Aesir (associated with war) and Vanir (associated with nature). Each group has its own strong representatives and together they contributed to the common peace. However, this was achieved only after the bloody Aesir-Vanir war.

Aesir

Gods from Aesir are considered as the main Gods. Its most known representatives are Odin, Frigg (or Freya*), Tyr, Thor, Baldur, Loki, Jord and Heimdall. They live together in Asgard and try to maintain peace and order within all words.

*there is a conflict whether Frigg and Freya are or aren't the same person, I am personally more inclined to the version where they are, as it gives more sense to many stories

Vanir

The second group of Gods - Vanir, were associated mostly with nature and fertility. Their main representatives are Freya, Freyr, Njord, and Nerthus. They lived in Vanaheim - one of the nine worlds. It is not certain where exactly this place lies, as it is barely mentioned in the sources, but it is definitely not as highly placed as Asgard is.

The Most Important Gods of Norse Mythology

Odin

Odin is also knows as All-father, as he is the main God in the whole Mythology, ruler of Asgard and the king of Aesir. He is considered as a God of wisdom, poetry and war and he has at least five children, from which the most popular one is Thor. He is often depicted without an eye, with a long beard, wearing a hat or a hood (that was due to his wandering around the world) and with Huginn and Munnin on his shoulders. We can also see him riding an eight-legged horse called Sleipnir - one of Loki's children.

Freya (Frigg)

Freya was the most important and the most popular Goddess among both the Gods and ordinary people. She is a member of both Vanir and Aesir. She was a wife of Odin, the Goddess of love, fate, beauty and fertility. In the stories she is often depicted as a beautiful promiscuous woman that can practice magic, to which she introduced other Gods as well. She drove a chariot drawn by two cats and wore a cloak made of falcon feathers. Due to her magic abilities and her association with the destiny, she was the most powerful Goddess in the Norse Mythology.

Thor

Thor is one of the most powerful Gods, also known as a Thunder God. That is due to his hammer - Mjolnir - with which he can control the lightning. This weapon is also considered as the most powerful in cosmos. He also possess a special belt of strength, that makes his already unmatchable physical strength even greater. He drove in his goat-driven chariot across the world and killed giants and often helped people. His biggest enemy is Jormungandr, as he kills Thor during Ragnarok.

Baldur

Son of Odin and Frigg (Freya), loved by all Gods, Goddesses and many other creatures, and immune from being harmed. He is one of the most beautiful and most cheerful among the deities. In sagas he is often said to give off the light. However, many of them are only about his death. His only weakness is a mistletoe, what Loki used to killed him. Apart from his beauty, he was very intelligent - he often dealt with disagreements between humans and Gods.

Loki

Loki is a blood brother of Odin, big trickster and shapeshifter. He is also known as the Father of the Monsters, as he often changed his body and gave birth to, for example, Sleipnir or Fenrir. In the stories he sometimes helps Gods and giants, but more often harm them. He is responsible for the death of God Baldur and is a father of the Goddess Hel - the ruler of the underworld.

Tyr

The God of justice and war, considered the bravest God of them all. Almost every time he was on the winning side during the battles. His great intelligence brought law in the cosmos and he often had the biggest voice in many arguments in the world of Gods. He later lost his hand in the fight as it was bitten off by the wolf Fenrir.

About me
