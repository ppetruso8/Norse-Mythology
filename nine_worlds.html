There are nine realms in the Norse Mythology, spreading out from the majestic tree Yggdrasil, that is in the centre of the universe. Some are on the branches, some in the roots. Their names are Asgard, Alfheim, Vanaheim, Midgard, Muspelheim, Niflheim, Jotunheim, Helheim, and Svartalfheim. Each realm has its own different creatures. Due to the lack of sources about the Norse Mythology, some of the realms are not described clearly nor even the position is provided, but we can still figure out a lot from the poems written by Snorri Sturluson.

Asgard

Asgard is the realm where group of Gods called Aesir lives. It is depicted as a beautiful place full of life and deities, surrounded by a great wall, and it should be placed at the top of the Yggdrasil. We can also find Valhalla, the hall of fallen warriors, there. Asgard is connected to the Midgard by the Bifrost bridge made of rainbow.

Alfheim

Alfheim is a land of elves, both light and dark ones. It is not far from Asgard, as it is still in heavens. However, the description of this realm is barely mentioned in the texts. We can assume there was a forest, because that is simply what elves prefer, and a beautiful nature, as the elves are famous for their beauty.

Vanaheim

Vanaheim is another world that's appearance is not clear. We know that Vanir live there and that it might be some kind of magical place due to Freya's powers. And assuming that the Vanir are famous for their fertility abilities, the land should be rich of many different kinds of plants and animals.

Midgard

Midgard (or the Middle Earth) is a place where humans live. It is surrounded by an ocean and Jormungandr. It is also protected by a wall, created from the eyelashes of the giant Ymir. That is because humans are very fragile and giants and other creatures would have no problem destroying them. It's first habitants were Ask and Embla, from which the whole humanity originates. However, the destiny of the Midgard is bounded to Ragnarok, as it should be destroyed. Its lands and seas will be poisoned.

Muspelheim

Muspelheim is one of the first created realms, formed from a vast void Ginnungagap. It is a land of fire, that is ruled by a giant Surtr. Its position is definitely under the Midgard, therefore somewhere near the bottom of Yggdrasil. There is basically nothing else but lava and flames. It is a home for the fire giants and fire demons.

Niflheim

Niflheim is another of the first created realms - the land of mist and cold. However, no one lives there. It should have a place somewhere near the bottom of the Yggdrasil, as well as the Muspelheim, but closer to the Helheim.

Jotunheim

A place close to both the Asgard and the Midgard. Jotunheim is a land of giants. It is depicted as a realm full of mountains and darkness without a fertile ground. However, there is a river that divides it from Asgard, that never freezes. Giants tend to fish there, as its their only source of food, apart from the forest animals. An interesting thing is, that Loki was born/created there.

Helheim

The Realm of Hel, also known as simply Hel, is an underworld, a place of dead, that are unworthy of Valhalla. Its ruler is the Goddess Hel - a daughter of Loki. Those, who end up in this dark cold place will never experience joy or happiness again. Its position is at the roots of Yggdrasil. To get there, one must cross a dangerous river full of weapons and proceed through the only gate present. There is no other way in, as it is surrounded by a huge wall.

Svartalfheim

Svartalfheim is the realms of dwarves, the highly skilled craftsmen of the Old World. They live in mountains, caves or under the surface. Apart from them, there is nothing, just darkness, smoke from their forges, and sound of hammers or other equipment.

About me

As you can see, my course is not related to the topic of this website, but I spent a lot of time doing research on the Viking Age, as it is my favorite historic period. The whole Mythology became my passion when I was on the primary school. My brother introduced me to the TV Series Vikings and I immediately fell in love. Since then, I am constantly developing my knowledge, and the more I know, the more I find it interesting and beautiful. I still have a lot of ground to cover, but I am already confident that this passion will stay with me throughout my whole life.
